New York COVID-19 Cases

Since March 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic has been a prevalent and dangerous presence around the world. The state of New York has been one of the epicenters for the virus in the U.S. during the pandemic. This chart, displaying the amount of new cases in New York City and the rest of New York State, represents the rising and falling of cases between March 2020 and July 2021. In the spring of 2020, April was the month with the most new cases as people were first learning what COVID was and how dangerous it could be. Case numbers didn't get that high again until January 2021, which saw the highest rise in cases during the time period represented.
